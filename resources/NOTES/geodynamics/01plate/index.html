    Plate Tectonics

    The World Atlas names seven major plates:

    1. African
    2. Antarctic
    3. Eurasian
    4. Indo-Australian
    5. North American
    6. Pacific
    7. South American.

    How to define a plate vertically & horizontally?

    1. Tectonic plates are gigantic pieces of the Earth's crust and uppermost mantle, i.e. pieces of the lithosphere. (vertically)
    2. The lithosphere, which is the rigid outermost shell of a planet (the crust and upper mantle), is broken into tectonic plates.
    3. moving as a whole (horizontally)

    The types of plate boundaries

    1. convergent
    2. divergent
    3. transform

    how to calculate plate motion ?

    1. current motion: GPS & InSAR
    2. historical motion: motion of paleo-magnetic dipole

    what is Wilson Cycle ?

    The Wilson Cycle is a model that describes the opening and closing of ocean basins and the subduction and divergence of tectonic plates during the assembly and disassembly of supercontinents.

    Phases of Wilson cycle: 1. From ten o'clock position clockwise: (10) initial pre-drift extension, 2. (12) rift-to-drift phase, initial opening of an oceanic basin, 3. (2 and 4) seafloor spreading, widening of the basin, 4. (6) subduction of oceanic lithosphere, closure of the basin, 5. (8) continent-continent collision

    evidence for plume hypotheisis:

    mantle plume hypotheisis is a nice theory to explain the observed hotspot and tracks. A supplementary to plate tetonics theory.
    the characteristics of hotspot:
    1. high heat flow (hotspots) 2. high swell topography 3. hotspot track
