    Geodynamics in 2021 spring, lectured by Prof. Leng

    terminology

    1. Geodynamics: a subfield of geophysics dealing with dynamics of the Earth. It applies physics, chemistry and mathematics to the understanding of how mantle convection leads to plate tectonics and geologic phenomena such as seafloor spreading, mountain building, volcanoes, earthquakes, faulting and so on.

    overview

    Geodynamics is generally concerned with processes that move materials throughout the Earth. In the Earth's interior, movement happens when rocks melt or deform and flow in response to a stress field.

    deformation of rocks

    elastic: < yield strength & << relaxation time

    The magnitude of the stress cannot exceed the yield strength of a material, and the time scale of the stress cannot approach the relaxation time of the material. If stress exceeds the yield strength of a material, bonds begin to break, which can lead to ductile or brittle deformation.

    ductile: > yield strength & = relaxation time

    Ductile deformation happens when the temperature of a system is high enough so that a significant fraction of the material microstates are unbound. Examples include bending of the lithosphere under volcanic islands or sedimentary basins, and bending at oceanic trenches.

    brittle: > yield strength & < relaxation time

    When strain localizes faster than these relaxation processes can redistribute it, brittle deformation occurs.

    Thermodynamics

    The most important thermodynamic variables are temperature and pressure. Within the upper lithosphere, brittle deformation is common. After the brittle-ductile transition zone, ductile deformation becomes dominant.

    Forces

    The main motive force behind stress in the Earth is provided by the energy from radioisotope decay, friction, and residual heat. Negative thermal buoyancy of the oceanic plates is the primary cause of subduction and plate tectonics, while positive thermal buoyancy may lead to mantle plumes, which could explain intraplate volcanism.

    Methods

    Data includes field observations, geodesy, radiometric dating, petrology, mineralogy, drilling boreholes and remote sensing techniques.

    Numerical modeling

    Forward modeling to:

    1. reproduce a specific observation
    2. produce basic fluid dynamics

    Basic fluid dynamics modelling can further be subdivided into instantaneous studies, which aim to reproduce the instantaneous flow in a system due to a given buoyancy distribution, and time-dependent studies, which either aim to reproduce a possible evolution of a given initial condition over time or a statistical (quasi) steady-state of a given system.

    Basic SI unit & typical constants

    1. density: 3000; 2700; 1000
    2. tectonic stress: 10 Mpa (1 pa = 1 N/m2; 1 Mpa = 1e6 pa; 1 Mpa = 10 bar)
    3. Young's Modulus for typical rocks: 70 Gpa
    4. magnitude for strain in the lithosphere: 1e-4
    5. Possion's ratio: \(\nu=0.25\)
    6. heat flux: a flow of energy per unit of area per unit of time. 65 +- 1.6 mWm-2 for continent; 101 +- 2.2 mWm-1 for ocean
    7. thermal conductivity k: a measure of its ability to conduct heat. Wm-1K-1
    8. heat capacity: the amount of heat to be supplied to an object to produce a unit change in its temperature.
