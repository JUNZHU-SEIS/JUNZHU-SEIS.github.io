    07OceanicLitho

    Updated Date: JUN 23, 2021

    description

    1. isostasy hypotheis: density of continental lithosphere is lower than that of oceanic lithosphere, so continental lithosphere can float on the oceanic lithosphere.

    2. the difference between oceanic & continental crust:

    type density thickness age composition
    basalt 3 5-7 young Fe & Mg
    granite 2.7 30-50 old Ca & Al & Na & K
    1. layering of the oceanic lithosphere & continental lithosphere & oceanic crust.
    type 1st layer 2nd layer 3rd layer *4th layer
    oceanic lithosphere sediments (0.6) sima (硅镁层 5.8) ultra mafic (34)
    continental lithosphere sediments (2.3) sial (硅铝层 16.8) sima (9)
    oceanic crust sediments pillow lava or sheeted dikes isotropic gabbro (upper) & laminar gabbro (lower)

    1. spreading rate of mid-ocean ridge: 0.5-10 cm/yr
      slow: thicken rift; fast: mid-ocean volcano

    2. seafloor bathymetry types: mid-ocean ridge (spreading center); ocean basin; marginal arc; trench

    3. describes the 2 different MORB generation models:

    4. infinite onion (fast spreading)

    5. infinite percolation (slow spreading)

    6. hydrothermal circulation
      ... to be updated

    7. Ophiolites: a section of Earth's oceanic crust and the underlying upper mantle that has been uplifted and exposed above sea level and often emplaced onto continental crustal rocks.

    8. difference between phase 1 and phase 2 of magma:
      phase 1 corresponds to MORB, with low NB and Ta
      because phase 1 of magma is from mid-ocean ridge, phase 2 of magma is from partial melt in subduction zone

    9. structure of the oceanic crust: basement & layer

    10. what affects the partial melt in the MORB ?
      seafloor spreading rate: seafloor spreading \(\rightarrow\) mantle material upwelling \(\rightarrow\) decompression melt \(\rightarrow\) oceanic crust
      F increases with (Po - Pf), where F denotes melt content, Po denotes the depth in which melt starts, Pf denotes the depth in which melt ends.
      the faster the mantle upwells, the shallower the Pf is, so as the (Po - Pf). Consequently, the larger F (熔体偏向于长英质，镁的含量降低) gives more melt and thicker oceanic crust.
    11. melting point of rocks: 1200 K - 1300 K

    terminology

    1. seamount: a large geologic landform that rises from the ocean floor but that does not reach to the water's surface
    2. magma chamber: a large pool of liquid rock beneath the surface of the Earth. About 1200 deg extrusive temperature.

    how

    1. how to resolve the composition of the oceanic crust ?
      seismology & drill & sample rocks

    explanation

    1. why the oceanic crust is thinner than continental crust ?
      according to isostasy theory: mountain range is higher than the water level, so the root is deeper than the anti-root; the density of the oceanic crust is larger than that of continental crust
