    06ContinentalLitho

    Updated Date: JUN 23, 2021

    terminology

    1. mantle wedge: a triangular shaped piece of mantle that lies above a subducting tectonic plate and below the overriding plate.

    description

    1. layering of the Earth's interior:
    depth interval PREM
    0-80 lithosphere
    0-10 upper crust
    10-20 lower crust
    20-80 LID
    80-220 Asthenosphere
    400-600 transition zone
    600-670 transition zone
    670-2890 lower mantle
    670-770 uppermost-lower
    770-2740 mid-lower
    2740-2890 D double prime
    2890-5150 outer core
    5150-6370 inner core
    1. crust types: oceanic (5-15 km); continental (30-50 km); transitiion zone (Conard discontunity, 15-30 km)
    thickness composition type
    30-50 \(SiO_2\), \(Al_2O_3\) continental
    5-15 \(MgO\), \(CaO\) oceanic

    1. layering of the continental crust:
    depth interval temperature properties
    upper continental crust low strong & brittle
    upper-lower boundary 15 km high seismicity
    lower continental crust high weak & plastic

    1. Zircon dating (30% in crust younger than 450 Ma) characteristics
      Uranium (shorter half-life than that of Zircon) can give higher accuracy; Zircon is more stable; high hardness; repeating melt contaminates the original Zircon

    2. evolution of the continental crust:

    3. An influx (涌入) from the descending slab of upward moving volatiles
    4. Some melt rising from deeper parts of the slab

    5. Driving of convective flow in the mantle wedge and moving the mantle material from the distant part of the wedge

    6. variation of element concentration from upper continental crust to lower one:
      K, U, Th, Si is decreasing while Fe, Mg is incresing.
      for radioactive elements, their melting point is relatively low; for Fe, Mg, their density is high so difficult to be conveyed to upper crust; for Si, due to component change.

    type composition
    crust quartz, feldspar
    mantle pyroxene, olivine
    1. characteristics of continental rift zone (大陆裂谷): high heat flow; thinning crust; low density anomaly in mantle (Rifting is the process by which the continental lithosphere stretches. These zones have important consequences and geological features, and if the rifting is successful, lead to the formation of new ocean basins.)

    1. why lower crust & oceanic crust lack incompatible elements ?
      subducted slab \(\rightarrow\) melt \(\rightarrow\) incompatible elements upwells with volitales
    2. incompatible elements is more rich in continental crust ?

    3. why concentration of incompatible elements decreases with the depth ?
