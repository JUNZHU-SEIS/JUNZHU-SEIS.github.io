    terminology (May 27, 2021)

    1. D: the lower mantle starting at 1000-km depth
    2. D': mesosphere (between 1000-km and D")
    3. D": the lowermost mantle
    4. MORB: midocean-ridge basalts
    5. Ma: million years
    6. PKJKP: seismic wave that traverses the IC as a shear wave
    7. P'P': wave that goes through the core and reflects off of the opposite side of the earth

    Preface and Philosophy (May 27, 2021)

    1. object: a better understanding of origin, structure, composition and evolution of our planet
    2. multidisciplinarity is more essential than ever
    3. seismologist: apt to interpret velocity anomalies in terms of temperature variations in a homogeneous, isotropic model
    4. petrologists: variations in mineralogy, crystal orientation or partial melt content counts more than temperature in the seismic velocity
    5. isotope geochemists: 650-km discontinuity, tend to interpret the chemical data in terms of ancient isolated reservoirs; exotic basalts are assumed to be from the CMB; MORB are viewed as a unique component of the mantle, rather than as an average composition of a heterogeneous population.
    6. convection theoretician: treats the mantle as a homogeneous fluid or as a 2-layered system, driven by temperature-induced buoyancy.
    7. Bias: rocks and magmas only represent shallow mantle and crust; seismic data is controlled by many factors, not only temperature-density
    8. current Earth paradigms from different disciplines: seismologists prefer a whole-mantle convection, while geochemists prefer layered convection
    9. deep-mantle features may be convectively isolated from upper-mantle processes.

    part 2: a dynamic planet (May 27, 2021)

    Plates are separated by boundaries and meet at triple junctions. In view of thermodynamics, the plates are driven by the thermal and density variations in the mantle. Cooling plates and sinking slabs can also be regarded as driving themselves, and driving convection in the underlying mantle. The cold stiff outer shell of earth is the active element and the template; the underlying convective mantle is passive. Ridge-trench-slab system is the main plate tectonic cycle.
