    11Heat

    Updated Date: JUN 23, 2021

    terminology

    1. geotherms: temperature depth profile

    description

    1. heat generation and transfer: control a variety of physical chemical process, such as volcanism, earthquakes, mountain building, plate motion.
      process in the lithosphere and asthenosphere; development of the Earth's basic structure; differentiation
    2. heat transfer: conduction (lithosphere & inner core); convection (mantle & outer core); radiation (hot lavas in the mantle); advection (laminar flow caused by plate motion)
    3. factors that control the Earth's temperature:
      outer factor (heat flow, surface temperature, erosion)
      inner factor (heat capacity, density, conductivity, radioactive generation)
    4. 2 mechanisms of oceanic heat flow: conduction & water circulation
    5. continental heat flow:
      characteristics: difficult to model due to the effects of geologic process (erosion, glacial); no general distribution law; heat flow decreases with the age.
      \(H_{continent}=H_{decay}+H_{mantle}\)
    6. temperature gradient
    type gradient
    crust 15 deg/km
    mantle 0.4 deg/km
    core 0.3 deg/km
    7. why adiabatic process in the mantle ?
    under the isotherm curve, material' entropy is fixed.

    boundary temperature
    surface 300 K
    MORB 1590-1750 K
    410 km 1760 K
    660 km 1870 K
    CMB 4080 \(\pm\) 130 K
    ICB 5650 \(\pm\) 600 K
    9. light elements will affect the melting point of iron

    explanation

    1. how does the inner core form ?
      not solidified from CMB, but due to: chemical differentiation; secular crystallization of inner core
    2. heat budget: secular cooling of mantle, heat flow of core (crystallization, cooling, differentiation, radioactive decay), tide friction
