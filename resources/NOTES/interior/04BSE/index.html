    terminology:

    1. BSE (bulk silicate Earth): Earth accreted from chondrites. Chemical elements classified as lithophile, siderophile, or volatile. Siderophile elements are partitioned into the core; Lithophile elements are partitioned into the silicate shell (depleted mantle & primitive mantle & oceanic crust & continental crust); Volatile elements lost to space or to atmosphere-ocean.
    2. depleted mantle: the part of Earth’s mantle from which basaltic melt has been extracted in one or multiple melting events at, for example, mid-ocean ridges, hot spots, or island arcs. Elements that do not fit into the crystal lattice of mantle minerals, the so-called incompatible elements, are preferentially incorporated into the melt and thus removed from the mantle, leaving the latter depleted with respect to these elements.
    3. primitive (primary) mantle: in geochemistry, a hypothetical reservoir with the composition of the Earth's crust and mantle taken together. (hypothetical undifferentiated mantle during the Earth's accretionary phase)
